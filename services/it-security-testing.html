Cyber Security Testing

Find your weaknesses before the hackers do

Small businesses are increasingly targeted by cybercriminals — yet most have never had their systems tested for vulnerabilities. IT security testing gives you an honest picture of how exposed your business really is, so you can fix problems before they become costly breaches. From penetration testing and Wi-Fi audits to phishing simulations and password policy reviews, I provide practical, jargon-free security assessments tailored to small businesses in NW London. You'll receive a clear report with prioritised, actionable recommendations — not a document full of scare tactics or unnecessary upselling.

Security Tests & Assessments Available:

  • Penetration Testing (PEN Test) — Simulated attack on your network, devices, or web systems to uncover real vulnerabilities before a malicious actor does
  • Wi-Fi & Network Security Audit — Check for open ports, weak encryption, rogue devices, and misconfigured routers
  • Phishing Simulation — Test how your staff respond to realistic phishing emails and identify training needs
  • Password & Access Policy Review — Assess password strength, reuse, and whether multi-factor authentication is properly in place
  • Device & Software Vulnerability Scan — Identify outdated software, unpatched systems, and insecure configurations across your computers
  • Email Security Check — Review SPF, DKIM and DMARC records to prevent your domain being used for spam or spoofing
  • Cloud & Remote Access Review — Audit Microsoft 365, Google Workspace, VPNs, and remote desktop setups for security gaps
  • Physical Security Assessment — Check for unlocked screens, unsecured devices, and physical access risks in your premises
  • Post-Test Remediation Support — Help implementing the fixes identified, not just handing you a report and walking away

Why Small Businesses Need Security Testing:

  • 43% of cyberattacks target small businesses — yet most assume they're too small to be a target
  • A single data breach can cost thousands in recovery, legal fees, and lost business
  • Many business insurance policies now require evidence of security due diligence
  • GDPR obligations mean a breach involving customer data can result in regulatory fines
  • Remote working has opened up new attack surfaces that many businesses haven't reviewed
